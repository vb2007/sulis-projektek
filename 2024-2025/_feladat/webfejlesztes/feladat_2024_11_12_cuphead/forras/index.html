About the game

Cuphead is a 2D side-scrolling run-and-gun video game created by brothers Chad and Jared Moldenhauer under Studio MDHR, featuring artwork inspired by 1930s cartoons. It was released on September 29, 2017, for Xbox One and PC. Following the game's success, it was adapted into a Netflix-exclusive series titled The Cuphead Show!, which premiered on February 18, 2022.

In Cuphead, one or two players control the animated characters Cuphead and his brother Mugman as they battle through various levels, each ending with a boss fight, all in an effort to repay their debt to The Devil. The game was widely praised for its unique art style and became known for its challenging difficulty. It achieved both critical and commercial success, winning multiple awards and selling over four million copies by July 2019.

Gameplay

Cuphead's gameplay centers around continuous boss battles, with occasional run-and-gun levels interspersed throughout. The game incorporates role-playing elements and features a branching level progression. Players have infinite lives and retain all equipment after each death. Coins collected from the run-and-gun levels can be used to purchase weapons and "Charms" (special abilities) from the in-game shop.

Players can use a parry attack on certain pink-marked objects, triggering various effects, with the most notable being an increase to the "super meter," which powers up stronger attacks. After completing a level, players receive a grade based on their performance, factoring in elements such as completion time, damage taken or avoided, and successful parries.

Levels are accessed through a top-down overworld, which also hides secret areas. The game includes a two-player local co-op mode, allowing a second player to control Mugman.
